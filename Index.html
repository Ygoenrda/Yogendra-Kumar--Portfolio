About Me

I am a passionate experimental physicist specializing in condensed matter physics, with a focus on curvilinear nanomagnetism, 2D magnetic nanostructures, and 3D magnetic systems. As a first-year PhD scholar in Spain, I explore the interplay between analytical calculations, numerical modeling, and experimental realization to advance curvilinear nanomagnetism, aiming to control topologically non-trivial structures and unlock new functionalities in nanomagnetic systems.

With a strong foundation in device engineering, I design experiments, analyze data, and interpret results with meticulous attention to detail. My research is driven by a relentless curiosity to explore novel problems and develop innovative solutions, pushing the boundaries of condensed matter physics.

Research

My work combines electronic and magneto-transport (AC & DC), magnetic thin film characterization, and advanced techniques like AFM, XRD, XRR, FMR, SEM, EDX, MPMS (SQUID), and PPMS for low-temperature and room-temperature measurements. I thrive at the intersection of theory and experiment, leveraging skills in analytical modeling, numerical simulations, and experimental design to tackle complex challenges.
